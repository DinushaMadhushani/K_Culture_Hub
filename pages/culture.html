The Essence of Korean Culture

A harmonious blend of ancient traditions and modern vitality

Values & Etiquette

Korean culture emphasizes respect for elders, family values, and harmonious social relationships. The concept of 'Jeong' represents deep emotional bonds and affection between people.

Festivals & Celebrations

From Seollal (Lunar New Year) to Chuseok (Harvest Festival), Koreans celebrate their rich heritage with traditional food, games, and ancestral rituals.

Arts & Crafts

Korean traditional arts include pottery, calligraphy, paper crafts (Hanji), and knot work (Maedeup). Each reflects Korea's aesthetic principles and craftsmanship.

Hanbok: Traditional Attire

The Hanbok is Korea's traditional clothing characterized by vibrant colors and simple lines. Worn for festivals, celebrations, and ceremonies, it reflects Korean aesthetics and social values.

  • Women's Hanbok consists of a jeogori (jacket) and chima (skirt)
  • Men's Hanbok includes a jeogori and baji (pants)
  • Colors indicate social status and marital status

Traditional Practices

Time-honored customs that continue to shape Korean society

Korean Tea Ceremony

The Korean tea ceremony, called Darye, is a traditional form of tea etiquette that represents harmony, respect, and tranquility. It's a spiritual practice that emphasizes mindfulness and appreciation of nature.

  • Focuses on green teas like Jeoncha and Ujeon
  • Emphasizes simplicity and naturalness
  • Reflects Korean appreciation for nature

Hanok Traditional Houses

Hanok are traditional Korean houses designed in harmony with nature. Built with natural materials like wood, stone, and clay, they feature the unique ondol heating system and elegant architectural lines.

  • Built using natural, eco-friendly materials
  • Ondol underfloor heating system
  • Designed for natural cooling in summer

Cultural Festivals

Celebrations that bring Korean traditions to life

Lunar New Year

Seollal

The Korean Lunar New Year, typically celebrated in January or February. Families gather to perform ancestral rites, wear hanbok, play traditional games, and share special foods like tteokguk (rice cake soup).

Harvest Festival

Chuseok

Often called Korean Thanksgiving, Chuseok is celebrated in autumn. Families gather to give thanks to ancestors, share food like songpyeon (rice cakes), and participate in folk games like ganggangsullae (circle dance).

Learn More
Spring Celebration

Buddha's Birthday

Celebrated on the eighth day of the fourth lunar month, this festival features lotus lantern parades, temple decorations, and free meals at Buddhist temples. The streets are filled with colorful lanterns.
